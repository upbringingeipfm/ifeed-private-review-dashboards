## PATTERN READOUT

### Cross-cutting themes (Week 25)

**Theme 1: AI governance is moving from principles to protocols with concrete regulatory deadlines.** EMA's Annex 22 target (Q4 2026) and MHRA's AI Airlock framework are the strongest signals. Organizations must shift from "monitoring AI" to "building AI governance infrastructure" with PCCPs, validation protocols, and data integrity controls.

**Theme 2: Data integrity enforcement remains global and unrelenting.** FDA's June 4 warning letters (Medline, Jaloma, HealthPartners, Aeroflex) continue to resonate as cautionary case studies. The common thread: data falsification, missing records, and inadequate computer system controls. Data integrity is foundational, not optional.

**Theme 3: RWE is becoming formal HTA infrastructure.** NICE's RWE framework operationalizes real-world evidence for reimbursement decisions, moving RWE from "supplemental" to "foundational" for market access.

**Theme 4: Political pushback on AI-driven cost containment.** The WISeR pilot vote signals that AI deployed for cost control without clinical guardrails will face resistance. Financial incentives matter and misalignment can kill a program.

### Most important QMS/quality-system intelligence opportunity

**EMA's Annex 22 (AI in GMP) and Annex 11 revision (Computerised Systems), both targeted for Q4 2026.** Quality leaders have 6-9 months to prepare for new requirements governing AI in GMP environments. Organizations should assess current CSV maturity, inventory AI/ML applications, and develop PCCP templates.

### Most important AI governance or clinical AI deployment signal

**MHRA AI Airlock Phase 2 findings on lifecycle management, PCCPs, and evolving human oversight.** For clinical AI teams, the insight that human oversight degrades over time as users trust AI outputs is critical. For AI governance, pre-market validation alone is insufficient — robust post-market monitoring is essential.

### Most important regulatory/evidence/HTA signal

**NICE RWE Framework's emphasis on target trial emulation and clinical relevance over statistical significance.** Operationalizes RWE for HTA in an actionable way. Evidence teams should study the framework's methodology for causal inference from observational data.
