## Proposed Final 9

1. FDA ML radiology software with PCCP enters Class II special-controls route.
   Why: converts AI-device lifecycle change control from guidance language into a codified classification pattern.

2. FDA Happiest Baby warning letter bridges old QS observations into live QMSR corrective-action expectations.
   Why: strongest W25 quality-system readiness signal for medtech operators.

3. EMA Management Board advances CTIS/Biotech Act readiness and PMS API operating infrastructure.
   Why: shows regulatory infrastructure being rebuilt before the legislative transition finishes.

4. DARWIN EU positions federated RWE as repeatable regulatory decision infrastructure.
   Why: supports the core theme that healthcare intelligence is becoming operating infrastructure.

5. HMA/EMA Alzheimer registry report turns disease-specific RWE into evidence-planning infrastructure.
   Why: connects registry governance, long-term safety/effectiveness, HTA and payer confidence.

6. EU HTA JSC update for high-risk devices and IVDs.
   Why: directly useful to medtech/IVD evidence teams preparing clinical investigations for JCA.

7. FDA breast biopsy needle supply disruption through Q1 2027.
   Why: operationally decision-relevant for diagnostic capacity, supplier risk and care-pathway continuity.

8. CMS ePA for Drugs comment window closes.
   Why: moves prescription-drug prior authorization API infrastructure toward final-rule territory and future payer transparency.

9. EMA platform approaches report expands reusable evidence logic beyond quality/manufacturing.
   Why: continuation of prior-knowledge and reusable-evidence themes into clinical/nonclinical development.

## Keep As Alternatives / Watch

- FDA AI-enabled device list / foundation-model tagging: valuable, but the future tagging implication should be phrased carefully.
- FDA READI-Home challenge: strong home-care innovation signal, but less urgent than supply/evidence/regulatory infrastructure.
- FDA Tzield approval: important evidence/accelerated-approval story; may be too product-specific unless framed as biomarker/surrogate-evidence precedent.
- MHRA PATHWAYS update: sensitive clinical-governance story; use only with careful wording and no policy overclaim.
- Medline recall expansion: useful QMS/postmarket quality signal; can support the warning-letter story.
- Anvisa GMP/seizure and certification-flow items: good global quality intelligence; may fit a separate global-regulator lane.
- Biogen-RayThera: market signal, but less aligned with operating-infrastructure theme.

## Exclude From Final 9 For Now

- Routine OTC naloxone/generic access items unless the issue needs a public-health access lane.
- Unconfirmed or rumor-like M&A.
- Older foundational guidance unless a W25 event activates it.
- Ireland/India ecosystem items unless kept in separate ecosystem lanes.
